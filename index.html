The Problem Worth Solving

Your teenager is being shaped by their thinking every single day.

Most teens spend hours absorbing messages about who they should be, how they should look, and what makes a life valuable — with zero tools to examine any of it. The result shows up as anxiety, comparison, fragile identity, and reactivity.

Perspective Lab gives them the tools to change that. Not therapy. Not another self-help course. Practical, faith-rooted, live skill-building in a small group led by someone who genuinely knows teenagers.

  • Proactive — before the crisis, not after
  • Faith-integrated throughout — never preachy
  • Small group — max 10 - 12 students for real depth
  • Live with a consistent facilitator, not pre-recorded, Parent attends first class with child
  • Students leave with a personal Perspective Playbook

Is This the Right Fit?

Made for the teenager who is ready to think.

✅ Perspective Lab is a great fit if your teen...
  • Is navigating normal adolescent challenges — comparison, anxiety, identity questions, peer pressure
  • Is curious, reflective, or willing to engage in honest conversation
  • Comes from a faith-oriented family and is open to Scripture as a foundation
  • Would benefit from a structured framework for how they think and respond
  • Thrives in small groups and does well with one-on-one adult mentorship
  • Is in a stable enough place to engage reflectively (not in active crisis)
This program is not the right fit if your teen...
  • Is currently experiencing an active mental health crisis requiring clinical support
  • Needs one-on-one therapeutic intervention rather than group learning
  • Is strongly resistant to any faith-based content or small group conversation
  • Is not open to exploring different ways of thinking about their experiences.

This program is not therapy — and that's intentional.

Perspective Lab is education and proactive development. It is not a substitute for clinical mental health support. If your teenager would benefit from therapy, we encourage you to pursue that as well. These two things can absolutely coexist.

Questions

Everything you need to know.

Perspective Lab is designed for proactive development — not crisis intervention. The ideal student is a curious, reflective 13–15 year old navigating normal adolescent challenges. The program is most effective when students are stable enough to engage in reflective conversation, not when they're in active crisis. Thriving teens benefit enormously from these tools, and so do teens who are quietly struggling.

A little initial resistance is normal and okay — many teenagers warm up quickly once they're in the room with peers who are engaging honestly. That said, a student who is deeply resistant to group conversation or any faith-based content is probably not a good fit right now.

Each session is anchored in one Scripture verse that deepens the core tool being taught — but the tone is always curious and inviting, never preachy or performative. The content stands on its own practically, and the faith layer underpins it.

Each session includes direct teaching, group discussion with guided questions, a hands-on worksheet activity, and a real-life practice challenge for the week. Students are expected to participate — share, reflect, respond. The small cohort size (max 10 - 12) means there's nowhere to hide, which is part of what makes it work.

Each session builds on the previous one, so regular attendance is important. One missed session is workable — I'll send the worksheet and a brief summary. Two or more missed sessions is difficult for the student and disrupts the group.

A parent is welcome and encouraged to attend the first session. Subsequent sessions are for students only. After each session I send a brief parent email with the core concept covered and a reflection prompt you can use with your teenager at home.
